The Problem We See

A major problem for enforcement teams is to know where and when to patrol in order to plan effective rounds. Existing techniques are based on traditional statistics like averages per hour, day, or zone. However, this approach focuses on a simple historical analysis of the data taken out of context and out of the dynamic environment of the city. Moreover, the amount of data required is prohibitive to work on in an per case scenario, and the calculations by hand are tedious, unreplicable, subject to miscalculation, and misinterpretation.

How Park.AI helps

To overcome these constraints, Park.AI utilizes machine learning algorithms to predict where and when the number of violations is highest, and hence prescribe the areas to visit. The Algorithm is therefore able to determine the ideal route to optimize parking enforcement

The Power of AI

A major advantage of Park.AI, is the ability to accurately forecast and extrapolate the number of violations from a reasonable amount of historical observations, during a dynamic scenario of a day in the city. Moreover, the respective impacts of temporal and spatial variables can easily be decoupled, for recommendations by sector and period that are reliable, actionable, and operational on the field.
